The Broken Path: A Tale of Friendship,
Betrayal, and the Consequences of Hidden Secrets

Aftab was in a frenzy, frantically checking his watch as he sprinted to the interview venue. He was already running late, but he knew that if he could just make it in time, he could salvage the situation.

But as he turned the corner, he was suddenly grabbed from behind. A masked figure threw a sack over his head and dragged him into a nearby alleyway.

"What's going on?" Aftab shouted, struggling to free himself. "Let me go, I'm late for my interview!"

But the masked figure was unmoved. Aftab struggled and pleaded, but the masked figure was relentless. They tied his hands and feet, ensuring that he was completely immobilized.

"Please, I beg you, let me go!" Aftab cried. "You don't understand, I have an important interview!"

But the figure only laughed, their voice muffled by the mask. "You think I don't know that? You think I'm doing this for fun? You deserve this, Aftab. You deserve everything that's coming to you."

Hours passed, and Aftab began to despair. The interview would be long over by now, and there was no way he would get the job. Finally, the masked figure untied him and threw him to the ground.

"Go," the figure said. "You're free to go. But remember, this is only the beginning."

Aftab stumbled to his feet, his legs shaky from being tied up for so long. He made his way out of the alley, a look of disbelief on his face.

Aftab returned to his dorm room, his mind racing with questions. "Who would want to do this to me?" he wondered. He thought back to his time at university, trying to remember if he had any enemies. But he couldn't think of anyone who would want to sabotage his interview.

Aftab shook his head, still puzzled. "I just don't understand. Why me?"

For days, Aftab was haunted by the incident. He couldn't shake the feeling that he was being targeted, but he had no idea who was behind it. One night, he decided to go for a walk, hoping to clear his head.

But as he turned a corner, he saw a figure standing in the shadows. Aftab froze. "Who's there?" he asked, his voice shaky.

The figure stepped forward, and Aftab's heart nearly stopped. It was Vicky. Aftab was stunned, unable to speak. He couldn't believe that Vicky was standing in front of him.

"Vicky?" he finally managed to say. "What are you doing here?"

Vicky just stared at him, his eyes cold and unfriendly. "I think you know why I'm here," he said.

Aftab shook his head, trying to process what was happening. "I don't understand," he stammered.

Vicky smiled, a cruel smile that didn't reach his eyes. "Oh, I think you do."

Aftab tried to back away, but Vicky stepped forward, blocking his path. "You ruined my life," Vicky hissed. "And now it's your turn to pay."

Aftab felt a chill run down his spine. "I didn't mean to hurt you," he said, his voice trembling. "I'm sorry."

Vicky laughed, a cold, hollow laugh. "Sorry isn't good enough," he said. "You're going to regret what you did to me."

Aftab's mind was racing. He knew he had to get away from Vicky, but he was trapped. "Please, let me go," he begged. "I won't tell anyone about this, I swear."

Vicky sneered. "It's too late for that," he said. "You should have thought about the consequences before you ruined my life."

Vicky stepped forward, holding up the object in his hand. "This is what you said to me," he growled.

Vicky just laughed. "Oh, I'm going to enjoy this," he said. And then he raised the bolt cutters and brought them down with a sickening crunch.

Aftab screamed in pain as Vicky brought the bolt cutters down again and again. Blood poured from his wounds, but Vicky didn't seem to care. He just kept laughing, a mad, crazed laugh that echoed through the warehouse.

Aftab could feel himself growing weaker, his vision blurring. "Please," he whispered, his voice barely a whisper. "Please stop."

But Vicky just laughed again. "Not yet," he said.

Aftab's screams grew weaker and weaker until they were nothing more than a weak whimper. Vicky stood over him, his eyes gleaming with a twisted pleasure. "Look at you," he said. "Pathetic."

Aftab could feel himself slipping away, but he forced himself to stay conscious. "P-please," he begged. "I'm sorry."
